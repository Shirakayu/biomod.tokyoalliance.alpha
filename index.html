ABSTRACT

  DNA can be aggregated with complementarity, and liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) and gelation of DNA molecules have been observed depending on the concentration. In addition, DNA can bind to specific substances as an aptamer, and convert sequence information into proteins through transcription and translation. This would allow for more complex control of reaction field formation and specific trap and release of molecules, substances, and cells by phase separation. However, gelation with DNA having these sequences, resulting in a decrease in the density of the completed gel because of adding aptamer or gene sequence, making it unstable.
  We focused on RCA as a method to control the gelation of long-strand DNA, which has an accomplishment in both gelation and Origami structures. The goal of this project is to design and evaluate a device that unravels the origami-like structure in an input-dependent manner to release long-stranded DNA and form a gel. We named our device "DNA Mochi Device", which transitions from the nanoscale origami state to the micro- to millimeter-scale gel state, based on the characteristics of the "Mochi" rice cake, in which small grains of rice gather and swell softly. We, Tokyo Alliance in BIO-MOD, worked to design and assay this device.
